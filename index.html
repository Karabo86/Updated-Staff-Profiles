Prof. Sylvester Mpandeli

Senior Research Manager: Water Use

Prof. Sylvester Mpandeli is a highly accomplished and distinguished expert in the fields of the water-energy-food (WEF) nexus, climate change adaptation, and agricultural water management. He is ranked globally by Scholar GPS within the top 0.05% of scholars globally in the climate change adaptation, agricultural water management and water-energy-food nexus in 2024. Scholar GPS also ranked him within the top 0.04% of scholars on the African continent. In 2024, Prof Mpandeli has close ties with Global South institutions such as Chinese National Committee on Irrigation and Drainage (CNCID), China Institute of Water Resources and Hydropower Research (IWHR) and Tsinghua University (TU). He has won multiple awards both at national and at global levels including Watsave Management Awards, World Heritage Irrigation Structures Awards, Vice President Honoraire award, Best Paper Awards. Prof Mpandeli is affiliated with various universities in the country.

Research Managers

Mr. Bonani Madikizela

Research Manager

Mr Bonani Madikizela is a Research Manager at the Water Research Commission (WRC), a role he has held since 2009, with over 30 years of experience in water resource quality management. He has led nationally significant wetland research programmes, driving strategic investment in wetland protection, management, and restoration, with a strong emphasis on citizen science and community empowerment. He has advanced wetlands as Ecological Infrastructure, integrating them into landscape restoration, climate resilience, and green economy initiatives. His multidisciplinary work spans wetlands, rivers, dams, and estuaries. Bonani has strengthened academic capacity, influenced national monitoring and policy, received multiple awards, and elevated African wetland science globally through SER leadership.

STAKEHOLDER AND COMMUNICATION

Executive

Ms. Ndundu Sithole

Acting Executive: Stakeholder and Communication

Ndundu Sithole is the Head of Communications and Promotions at the Water Research Commission (WRC), a position she has held since September 2024. She is a highly accomplished media and communications strategist with more than 22 years of experience spanning broadcast journalism, newsroom leadership, corporate communications, and stakeholder engagement. Her distinguished career includes senior leadership roles at globally recognised and leading media organisations such as Thomson Reuters, SABC News, e.tv, and Newzroom Afrika. Throughout her career, she has led major national and international news coverage, managed high-performing newsroom teams, and secured exclusive interviews with prominent local and global figures. She was also part of the award-winning Reuters team that covered some of South Africa's most defining moments, including the passing of former President Nelson Mandela and the Marikana tragedy. At the WRC, she leads strategic communication, brand positioning, and stakeholder engagement initiatives aimed at strengthening public awareness, sector influence, and the impact of water research and innovation in South Africa. Her work focuses on translating complex research into accessible narratives that connect policy, people, and development outcomes. In 2019, on Women's Day, Ndundu successfully summited Mount Kilimanjaro alongside the Imbumba Foundation and the Nelson Mandela Foundation under the theme "Climb for Justice, Climb for Dignity", an experience that reflects her resilience, determination, and commitment to personal growth and purpose-driven leadership. Beyond her professional achievements, Ndundu is a foodie, an avid reader, and a passionate music enthusiast who enjoys cooking. She is currently completing her MBA at Henley Business School Africa.

ndundus@wrc.org.za
